Hey there, I am a PhD student advised by [Lawrence Kim](https://www.lhkim.com/) under [Tangent Lab](https://tangent.cs.sfu.ca/) at [Simon Fraser University](https://www.sfu.ca/). 

Previously, I got my BS degree in Computer Science at the [University of Wisconsin Madison](https://www.wisc.edu/), where I worked with [Yaxin Hu](https://www.edayaxin.com/about.html) in [People and Robots Lab](https://peopleandrobots.wisc.edu/), advised by [Bilge Mutlu](https://bmutlu.github.io/).

I came to research the way a lot of people come to obsessions: through science fiction. I am especially motivated by [Black Mirror](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Mirror) S1E3 [The Entire History of You](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Entire_History_of_You), where memory becomes a perfect, replayable, shareable recording. It poses a question I now work on seriously: what should it mean for a machine to remember with us?

My research develops collaborative agentic memory systems: agents that accumulate, share, and reason over memory across users and tasks, rather than treating it as a passive log. I'm interested in how memory gets formed, negotiated, and forgotten between multiple agents and the people they work with. Science fiction gave me the questions; a PhD is where I try to answer them. 

I am always open to new connections and potential collabrations, feel free to drop me an email!
